Style Tips for Men

Free. Honest. No fashion magazine fluff.

The Basics

If you only take one thing away: fit beats everything. A €20 shirt that fits looks better than a €200 one that doesn't.

Fit is king

Shoulder seams on your shoulders. Sleeves end at your wrist. T-shirt ends at belt height. Pants without fabric bunching at the ankle. It's that simple.

Less is more

10 pieces that go together are better than 50 random buys. Build a capsule wardrobe – fewer, versatile pieces that all work with each other.

Invest in shoes

Shoes are the first thing people notice about your outfit. One pair of clean minimal sneakers and one pair of smart leather shoes cover 90% of all situations.

Colors

The simplest rule: when in doubt, stick to neutrals. You almost can't go wrong combining them.

The safe foundation

Black, white, gray, navy, beige, and olive green. With these six colors you can build a complete outfit that always works.

One accent is enough

Want color? Then one. A burgundy hoodie with black pants and white sneakers. Not three bold colors at once.

Stay away from neon

There are exactly zero situations where a neon yellow t-shirt is the right call. Unless you're running a marathon. And even then.

Occasions

The trick: slightly overdressed beats underdressed. Nobody has ever been criticized for looking too good.

First date

Well-fitting dark jeans or chinos, a clean t-shirt or button-up (roll up the sleeves!), clean sneakers or Chelsea boots. Simple, but intentional.

Business casual

Chinos + button-up + blazer. No tie. Clean leather shoes. Done. This works in 95% of offices and every business dinner.

Weekend / casual

Well-fitting joggers (not the stretched-out gym ones!) or chino shorts in summer. Pair with a premium basic tee and clean sneakers.
